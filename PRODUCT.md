# Birdseye — Product Context

## Register

product — the app is a camera-first tool the user works *through*, not a marketing surface. Brand personality still shows (rounded, friendly, encouraging) but chrome must disappear into the task.

## Who / what / why

- **Users**: hands-on learners mid-task — origami, PCB assembly, woodworking, cooking — phone propped up or in one hand, eyes on their work.
- **Product**: live camera + voice AI learning coach. Speak a goal, get a step-by-step lesson narrated aloud with world-anchored AR annotations over the camera feed, and push-to-talk questions answered in context.
- **Outcome**: the learner finishes a real physical task with confidence. Success = steps completed, not time in app.

## Brand personality

Clean. Optimistic. Human. A patient expert, never a know-it-all. Coach, not lecturer: short sentences, second person, celebrates progress.

## Anti-references

- Loud SaaS dashboards; gradient-soaked hero chrome over a camera feed.
- Robotic system voices and machine-like copy.
- Anything that covers the camera: the feed is the surface, UI floats sparsely on top.

## Design principles

1. **Camera is the canvas.** Chrome floats as small cards and pills; the feed stays dominant.
2. **Legible over any feed**: overlays and text always carry a dark translucent pill or white card backing plus soft shadow.
3. **Voice-first**: the mic is the primary control on every screen; tap targets ≥ 44px for messy/busy hands.
4. **One warm accent per composition**; success is green, live/detected is signal green.
5. Visual specs (color, radii, type, components) live in `design.md` — the single source of truth.
